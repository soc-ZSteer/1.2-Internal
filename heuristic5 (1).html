5. Error Prevention

Short Summary

The fifth usability heuristic, Error Prevention, focuses on designing systems that help users avoid making mistakes before they happen. Instead of just showing error messages after something goes wrong, the system should guide users toward correct actions and make errors less likely. For example, a form might highlight missing information before submission or disable invalid options so users can’t select them. This proactive approach saves time and reduces frustration by preventing problems rather than just reacting to them. Overall, error prevention makes a system more reliable, user-friendly, and efficient.
