3. User Control and Freedom

Short Summary

The third usability heuristic, User Control and Freedom, means that users should always feel in control of the system and be able to easily undo or redo actions. People often make mistakes, so the system should provide clear options to go back, cancel, or correct errors without frustration. For example, having an “Undo” button, a “Back” option, or a confirmation step before deleting something important gives users a sense of safety and flexibility. This heuristic is about making sure users never feel trapped or powerless, allowing them to freely explore and interact with the system with confidence.
