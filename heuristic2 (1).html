2. Match Between System and the Real World

Short Summary

The second usability heuristic, Match Between System and the Real World, means that a system should use language, symbols, and concepts that are familiar to the user, rather than technical or system-based terms. It should follow real-world conventions so that information feels natural and easy to understand. For example, a shopping website might use a shopping cart icon because it mirrors a real-world action that users already understand. This helps users make sense of how the system works without needing special training. Overall, this heuristic focuses on making the digital experience intuitive by reflecting how people think and interact in the real world.
