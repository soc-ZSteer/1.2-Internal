6. Recognition Rather Than Recall

Short Summary

The sixth usability heuristic, Recognition Rather Than Recall, means that systems should make options, actions, and information visible so users don’t have to remember things from one part of the interface to another. It’s easier for people to recognise something they see than to recall it from memory. For example, showing a list of recently used files or providing menus with clear icons helps users quickly find what they need without having to remember exact names or commands. This heuristic reduces cognitive load and makes the system more intuitive and efficient to use.
