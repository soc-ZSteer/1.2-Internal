8. Aesthetic and Minimalist Design

Short Summary

The eighth usability heuristic, Aesthetic and Minimalist Design, means that a system’s interface should be simple, clean, and free of unnecessary information or visual clutter. Every element on the screen should have a clear purpose and help the user complete their task. Too much text, colour, or decoration can distract users and make it harder to focus on what really matters. For example, a website with clear headings, enough white space, and only essential buttons feels easier to use and more professional. This heuristic focuses on clarity and simplicity, ensuring the design looks appealing while staying functional.
